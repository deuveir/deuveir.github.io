LANGUAGE . OLD VANEAC 2015.01

Old Vaneac is a particle-based philisophical conlang created in the first verse of Vaneakeu. The structure of the language is simple, where words are built by attaching broad particles together to gain new meaning. Below is a list of these particles:

Xy - Alignment

Xa - Positive

Xe - Negative

Xi - Connect

Xo - Disconnect

Xu - Neutral

-

Ky - Modality

Ka - Simple

Ke - Complex

Ki - Possible

Ko - Impossible

Ku - Uncertain

-

Vy - Rationality

Va - Addition

Ve - Subtraction

Vi - Finite

Vo - Infinite

Vu - Nothing

-

Sy - Possess

Sa - Parent

Se - Location

Si - Child

So - Substitute

Su - Redirect

-

Ny - Action

Na - Do

Ne - Observe

Ni - Become

No - Control

Nu - Study

-

Ly - Material

La - Complex

Le - Organic

Li - Synthetic

Lo - Body

Lu - Soul

-

Fy - Define

Fa - Module

Fe - Inside

Fi - Outside

Fo - Together

Fu - Separate

Consonants are used to give a "theme" to the particle, while vowels are to give an "ability". Particles that end with a -y are the most broad of their "theme" and the rest follow to give modifications (IE "Ly" contains "Lu" but "Lu" does not contain "Ly", however "La, Le, Li, and Lo" are equal to "Lu"). To stress more meaning on an aspect of a word, particals can be repeated by their single consonants and vowels. An example can be the word "Xallu", which could have the meaning of "a good strong soul" for the "material" of "Lu" being repeated in the middle of positivity and soul. Sentences can be constructed in both linear and non-linear fashion as the nature of the script lets it - something that can be very flexible in representation. More examples will be added in time.
