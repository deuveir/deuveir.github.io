in this world, children are born physically as capable fetuses which sometimes can resemble a flesh spinal cord isopod. when children are born they possess a physical spirit working with their body in synchronization with their pure ideals and outlook on the world. form is not coherent for them, but as the children grow through adolescence their bodies more resemble typical humanoids. however, they still possess energy from the spirit as it grows with them. eventually the spirit is disconnected entirely with the body of the humanoid and exists in its own mirror spirit realm experiencing some similar mental state and whatever it was possessing. these are like literal “tulpas” that have been existing in the humanoid society for as long as there was written history of them. these spirit tulpas all sport unique designs and forms describing their incarnate state, which when the fetus children are growing up can shapeshift into at any time. it is very rare to see those still have their childhood tulpa abilities remaining as they grow into adulthood but are mostly taken care of in conspiracy systems which concentrate those groups of people into experiencing simulated sub-realities. this all changes when one individual, in their early 30s, is revealed to have somehow taken the tulpas of thousands of children into himself collecting such a strong force and set of abilities to invoke an apocalyptic-like event that would have the world be reset into living in tandem with the spirit tulpa word permanently. as this individual is revealed, more and more people begin fighting for this new cause to live in a literal fantasy with their youthful self forever and become a follower. political and societal chaos ensues. the protagonist of this story is a 13-year old humanoid with the ability of physical regeneration. their tulpa is a cute, seed-like being with a single large eye poking out from the inside. they are set into a form of government child army which has been concealed by aesthetics and location, and begin their fight against the force of dimensional collapse.
